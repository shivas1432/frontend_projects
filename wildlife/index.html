Animals are multicellular eukaryotes whose cells are bound together by collagen. Animals dominate human conceptions of life on Earth because of their size, diversity, abundance, and mobility. The presence of muscles and mobility is one of the primary characteristics of the animal kingdom.

Elephant: With their remarkable intelligence and gentle demeanor, elephants are the largest land animals, revered for their familial bonds and intricate communication methods.

Dolphin: Highly intelligent and social marine mammals, dolphins are known for their playful behavior, remarkable agility, and sophisticated communication through clicks and whistles.

Lion: Majestic and powerful, lions are apex predators known for their distinctive mane and social behavior in prides, making them iconic symbols of strength and royalty in many cultures.

Penguin: Well-adapted to life in icy regions, penguins are flightless birds known for their distinctive waddling gait, strong sense of community, and adept swimming abilities.
